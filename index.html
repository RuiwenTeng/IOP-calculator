Clinical Note: This model provides theoretical calculations based on first-order kinetics and population-averaged parameters for adult eyes with standard gas fill volumes. Not applicable to pediatric patients, axial length >26 mm, or glaucoma history. Actual IOP elevation may vary significantly due to individual ocular compliance, exact gas fill percentage, and measurement error. Patients with values near the 6 mmHg threshold (±1-2 days) require particularly cautious assessment. Clinical decisions should integrate slit-lamp examination of residual gas volume and applanation tonometry.
